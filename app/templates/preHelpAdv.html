Preprocessing Help

General

The preprocessing step uses statistics and natural language processing techniques to reduce spell errors and different word forms. This is realized through a similarity replacement algorithm which computes how similar two words are - and replaces the unimportant with important ones. Importance is defined by the formula you already provided. The threshold defines how many word will be replaced.

On top is a table with two lines, current and new. Current is a automaticaly created default and a good starting point. The threshold means how similar two words need to be replaced - 0.65: 65% similarity. Replacements shows how many words will be replaced with the given threshold.

Histogram

The histogram below the list shows the number of tags binned over importance values - 10 at the 0.1-0.2 bin means that there are 10 tags within the range 0.1 and 0.2. You can zoom in by dragging a window or clicking onto a bin.

Vocab Grid

This grid shows all words currently visible in the histogram above. If you click on a tag, the grid on the right side will show all possible replacements corresponding to this tag.

Replacements Grid

The grid shows all replacements for the selected word in the vocab grid. The new threshold will be updated, if you click on a tag.
